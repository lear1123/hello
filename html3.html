Definition of Streaming

....................

Streaming is a technique for watching videos and listening to audio content without already downloading the media assets. When you stream video or music, information is sent from a server in the form of a data stream. The decoder, which may be a standalone player or a browser plugin, operates as an integral component of the web browser.

Types of Streaming Content

Music:

Music streaming is listening to songs through apps or websites that provide them on the internet (ex. soundcloud, spotify). The service transports data to the user in small amounts so that they can receive pre-buffed music.

Movies:

Movie streaming is watching pre recorded videos through websites that provide this type of service (ex. Netflix, Hbo, Disney+). The media file for the movie is stored remotely on the client and is transmitted to the user a few seconds after a time in the internet.

Podcasts:

Podcast is an episodic series of spoken-word digital audio recordings that a user may download and listen to on a personal device. Streaming apps and podcasting services enable the management of a personal consumption queue across many podcast sources and playback devices in a simple and integrated manner..

TV Shows:

Television program is any content created for viewing on a television set that is aired over-the-air, through satellite, or via cable, omitting breaking news, commercials, or trailers that are usually interspersed between shows. Some television programs are often planned months in advance and are included in electronic guides or other television schedules, streaming services frequently make them accessible for watching at any time.

Streaming media

Streaming media is multimedia that is delivered and consumed in a continuous manner from a source, with little or no intermediate storage in network elements. Streaming refers to the delivery method of content, rather than the content itself.
